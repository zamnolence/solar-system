  Overview

Our solar system features eight planets, seen in this artist’s diagram. This representation is intentionally fanciful, as the planets are depicted far closer together than they really are.

The inner, rocky planets are Mercury, Venus, Earth and Mars. The outer planets are gas giants Jupiter and Saturn and ice giants Uranus and Neptune. Beyond Neptune, a newer class of smaller worlds called dwarf planets reign, including longtime favorite Pluto.

Thousands more planets have been discovered beyond our solar system. Scientists call them exoplanets (exo means "from outside.")

  Mercury

Mercury, a rocky planet, is a terrestrial planet that spins slowly on its axis. It is the smallest and closest planet to the Sun but not the hottest planet in the Solar System. Mercury's thin atmosphere is composed of oxygen, sodium, helium, hydrogen, and potassium, which is unbreathable.

  Venus

Venus, the terrestrial planet, is the 2nd closest planet to the sun. It spins slowly in the opposite direction. Its structure and size are similar to Earth. The atmosphere of Venus is known to be dense and toxic, which makes Venus the hottest planet in the Solar System.

⊕  Earth

Earth, a rocky terrestrial planet, is the 3rd solar planet from the sun. It is only the planet having an atmosphere containing free oxygen, oceans of water, which is perfect place for life! Earth completes one spin for 24 hours.

  Mars

Mars, the Red Planet, is the 4th planet from the Sun. Mars is one of the most explored planets in the Solar System in the belief that the planet is potentially habitable due to many reasons: Its surface consists of water and oxygen, the temperature is not too hot or too cold, there is enough sunlight, and the gravity is sufficient for human body to adapt to. However, it is still a tough place for life as the atmosphere is made up of carbon dioxide, argon, nitrogen.

♃  Jupiter

Jupiter, a gas giant planet, is the 5th planet from the sun and the largest in the Solar system. Jupiter's Great Red Spot is a gigantic torm that’s about twice the size of Earth and has raged for over a century. Its atmosphere is made mostly of helium and hydrogen, which does not support life.

♄  Saturn

Saturn, a gas giant planet with ring system, is a 2nd-largest planet and the 6th planet from the sun. Similarly to Jupiter, Saturn atmosphere is made up of helium and hydrogen, while its rings are made of moon dust, rocks and water ice. These conditions of Saturn makes it a lifeless behemoth.

⛢  Uranus

Uranus, the ringed, icy gaseous giant planet, is the 7th planet from the sun. Like Venus, Uranus rotates in the opposite direction to other planets. But Uranus is unique in that it rotates on its side! Uranus's atmosphere is made up of hydrogen and helium, with a small amount of methane, which cannot support life.

♆  Neptune

Neptune, a blue ice giant planet, is the 8th planet from the sun and the 4th-lagest planet. Like Uranus, its atmosphere is made up of hydrogen and helium, with a small amount of methane. It is dark, cold and vey windy.
